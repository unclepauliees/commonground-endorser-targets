01 · Methodology

The Commonground Endorser Fit Index

Every target on this list is scored on six dimensions — not ranked by fame. The CEFI is what makes this document defensible in a financial and partnership context: it separates Trust Anchors from Reach Amplifiers, and both from Niche Authorities.

01 · TD
Trust Density
Trust-weighted reach inside the target community, not raw follower count. A figure deeply trusted by a trust-gap community — Black 21%, Hispanic 18% — outranks a bigger but shallower celebrity. The foundational dimension of the Commonground thesis.
Weight × 2.0 → max 10 pts
02 · NE
Narrative Equity
Does the person already embody an authentic, publicly self-disclosed health or wellness story? Weight journey, chronic-condition advocacy, mental-health openness, maternal health, fitness transformation. "Founders, not faces" requires real narrative, not manufactured affinity. Only publicly voluntary disclosures count.
Weight × 1.5 → max 7.5 pts
03 · PA
Program Adjacency
Concrete fit to OpenLoop program lines: GLP-1 weight management, female sexual health, hormone optimization, men's TRT, mental & behavioral health, chronic care/metabolic, longevity & performance, women's/maternal health, dermatology-adjacent wellness. Score = depth × breadth.
Weight × 1.0 → max 5 pts
04 · CD
Cultural Durability
Legacy over clout. Career architecture, reputation durability, low volatility. Commonground wants trust anchors that compound over a 5–10 year horizon — not viral flashes. Institutional credibility, longevity of audience, and generational reach all contribute.
Weight × 1.0 → max 5 pts
05 · GS
Governance Safety
Inverse of governance risk — higher score means lower risk. Controversy exposure, FTC-readiness, reputational volatility, prior brand-safety incidents, controversy-clause enforceability. Score 5 = near-zero institutional risk; score 1 = high and present risk.
Weight × 1.5 → max 7.5 pts
06 · DA
Demographic Anchor
Which of the three markets the person authentically unlocks — and whether they expand coverage vs. duplicate an existing lane. Bilingual/community authenticity is additive. Pure duplication of an existing sub-brand partner's lane is penalized.
Weight × 1.0 → max 5 pts

Composite method: Weighted sum — TD(×2) + NE(×1.5) + PA(×1) + CD(×1) + GS(×1.5) + DA(×1). Maximum composite: 40.0 points. All scores represent research-informed estimates as of June 2026 and should be treated as directional, not actuarial.

A-List / Sub-Brand

Co-Founder Candidate

High Trust Density + high Narrative Equity + broad Program Adjacency across a full multi-program portfolio + high Cultural Durability + acceptable Governance Safety + plausible equity co-founder disposition. Rare by design. Reserved for figures who could realistically anchor a standalone brand at the scale of Cardi Care, King Wellness, Fenty Wellness, or Yeezy Health.

B-List / Endorsement

Channel Endorser

Strong on one or two CEFI dimensions — reach or niche credibility — but lacking the breadth, durability, or equity-readiness to anchor a standalone sub-brand. Unlocks a specific community, program vertical, or distribution channel. Signing fee + flat/performance compensation + FTC disclosure. No equity, no standalone brand.

Influencer / Creator

Niche Authority

Health/medical creators, fitness educators, bilingual lifestyle creators, community physicians-turned-creators. Frequently the highest Trust Density per follower in a specific community — the "niche authority" archetype punches far above raw reach. Endorsement, affiliate, or channel partnerships. Often the trust-per-dollar leaders in the portfolio.

Whitespace Rule

The four anchored sub-brands (Cardi Care, Yeezy Health, King Wellness, Fenty Wellness) already occupy defined lanes. Every A-list sub-brand candidate on this list must explicitly name the demographic or category whitespace they open. Proposals that simply duplicate an existing partner's lane are reclassified to B-list or removed.

Conflict Rule

Anyone contractually tied to a competing telehealth or wellness delivery brand is flagged as a validation reference, not an active target. Current contract status is noted where publicly verifiable. If a deal has lapsed or is unverifiable, that uncertainty is stated explicitly — a flagged gap beats a confident fabrication.

Anti-Fabrication Guardrail

Every health narrative cited in this document is publicly and voluntarily self-disclosed by the person referenced. No medical information has been inferred, invented, or implied. Where a figure's health narrative is undisclosed, that section is omitted rather than filled. All reach figures are estimates labeled with the research date.
